About Me

Hi! I'm Grant Li, a first-year electrical engineering student at the Georgia Institute of Technology with threads in Circuit Technology and Signal Processing and AI.

My interests and work range from analog circuit design and PCB layout to embedded programming and signal processing. I love the challenge of taking a concept from schematic to working prototype. I'm interested in contributing to open-source hardware projects, working on team projects involving research and development, and continuously learning about how new technology is driving engineering innovation.

Beyond my technical projects, I am passionate about supporting my local community through collaboration, leadership, and mentorship. Throughout high school, I was actively involved with volunteering, including being the cofounder and previous co-president of a club dedicated to providing volunteering opportunities to more youth. At Georgia Tech, I am an active member of the John H. Martinson Honors Program where I help organize and host events aimed to better support Honors students. I work closely with Honors staff and councils in the country as I aspire to enable younger students to grow professionally through conferences.

Looking ahead, I plan to specialize in analog electronics and signal processing, where I can apply my skills, continue shaping the chip design industry, and continue growing as an engineer. In my future time at Georgia Tech, I would like to build stronger connections with faculty through research, get involved with The HIVE Makerspace, and branch out professionally through internship positions.

I'm always looking to stay active outside of engineering through sports (mostly tennis, basketball, skiing), working out, and I would love to travel to more places in the near future.

Feel free to explore my portfolio and connect through my contacts below!

R&D Electromechanical Intern

Tartan Energy Group
My Experience

I had a great experience at Tartan Energy Group working alongside a talented team of engineers on real downhole tools. This internship gave me hands-on exposure to the full hardware development cycle, from designing and testing PCB assemblies to writing technical documentation, and solidified my passion for hardware engineering.

My Contributions
  • Thermal tested data receivers used in Measurement While Drilling (MWD) tools at temperatures of 150°C by measuring current across varying resistance levels with a multimeter to verify performance under varying downhole conditions
  • Developed and programmed a newly proposed rotation stand model for testing MWD tools, enabling tilt from 0–90 degrees with 360-degree rotation, effectively simulating directional drilling
  • Authored 3 technical reports and a documentation of stand instructions
